Frequently Asked Questions

What is the Difference Between a Passport and a Visa?

A passport is a government-issued document that verifies your identity and nationality, while a visa is a conditional authorization granted by a foreign country allowing you to enter, remain within, or leave that country.

What is the Difference Between an Entry Visa and a Tourist Visa?

An entry visa is a general term for a visa that allows you to enter a country for a specific purpose. A tourist visa is a type of entry visa specifically for tourism or recreational purposes.

What Is the Difference Between a Visa and a Residence Permit?

A visa allows you to enter a country for a temporary stay, while a residence permit allows you to live in a country for an extended period, usually for work, study, or family reunification.

What Are Electronic Travel Authorizations?

Electronic Travel Authorizations (ETAs) are online systems that allow travelers from eligible countries to visit a specific country without needing a traditional visa. They are often quicker and easier to obtain than visas.

What Are the Most Common Reasons for Visa Denial?

Common reasons for visa denial include incomplete applications, insufficient funds, lack of strong ties to your home country, security concerns, and previous immigration violations.
