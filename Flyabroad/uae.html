UAE Visa Types

Tourist Visa

How to Apply:

Generally, you can apply for a UAE tourist visa through an airline, a travel agency, or a hotel in the UAE. Some nationalities are eligible for visa-on-arrival. Please check the official website of the UAE Ministry of Foreign Affairs and International Cooperation or the Federal Authority for Identity, Citizenship, Customs & Port Security for the latest regulations.

Bank Statement Amount in PKR:

The required bank statement amount can vary depending on the duration of the visa and the specific requirements of the sponsoring entity. It's advisable to have a sufficient balance to cover your stay. While a specific amount in PKR isn't always explicitly stated, having a balance equivalent to approximately AED 10,000 - AED 20,000 (around PKR 750,000 - PKR 1,500,000 as of April 2025, subject to exchange rate fluctuations) for a one-month stay is generally recommended. Please confirm with the application authority.

Things that are Allowed:

  • Tourism and leisure activities.
  • Visiting friends and family.
  • Short-term business meetings (depending on the specific type).

Things that are Not Allowed:

  • Engaging in employment or any form of work for a UAE-based employer.
  • Overstaying the visa validity.
  • Conducting business activities without the appropriate visa.

Things to Remember:

  • Ensure your passport has at least six months of validity.
  • Carry copies of your visa and passport at all times.
  • Respect the local laws and customs of the UAE.

Other Important Details:

Tourist visas are usually valid for 30 or 90 days. Extension options may be available but are subject to approval. Medical insurance is often required.

Employment Visa

How to Apply:

An employment visa is typically sponsored by a UAE-based employer. The employer will initiate the application process on your behalf. This usually involves obtaining entry permits before your arrival.

Bank Statement Amount in PKR:

Generally, a personal bank statement is not the primary requirement for an employment visa application initiated by an employer. However, the sponsoring company might need to provide financial guarantees. If a personal bank statement is required in specific scenarios, the amount will be determined by the authorities. It's best to consult with your employer or the relevant UAE authorities.

Things that are Allowed:

  • Working for the sponsoring employer as per the employment contract.
  • Obtaining a residence permit after arrival.
  • Sponsoring eligible family members (subject to regulations).

Things that are Not Allowed:

  • Working for any employer other than the sponsor without proper authorization.
  • Violating the terms of your employment contract.
  • Overstaying your residence permit after job termination.

Things to Remember:

  • Ensure you have a valid employment contract.
  • Complete all required medical examinations and biometric procedures upon arrival.
  • Familiarize yourself with UAE labor laws.

Other Important Details:

The validity of an employment visa and subsequent residence permit is usually tied to your employment contract. You will need to undergo a medical fitness test in the UAE.

Residence Visa (Sponsored by Family)

How to Apply:

A resident of the UAE can sponsor their family members (spouse, children, parents) subject to meeting certain salary and other requirements. The sponsor needs to apply through the General Directorate of Residency and Foreigners Affairs (GDRFA) in the respective emirate.

Bank Statement Amount in PKR:

While a specific bank statement amount in PKR might not be explicitly mandated, the sponsor needs to demonstrate a minimum salary, which varies depending on the emirate and the family members being sponsored. As of April 2025, the minimum salary requirement is generally around AED 4,000 - AED 5,000 (approximately PKR 300,000 - PKR 375,000). Please verify the exact requirements with the GDRFA.

Things that are Allowed:

  • Living in the UAE with the sponsoring family member.
  • Accessing healthcare and education facilities.
  • In some cases, obtaining a work permit.

Things that are Not Allowed:

  • Working without a valid work permit if required.
  • Overstaying the visa validity.
  • Engaging in illegal activities.

Things to Remember:

  • Ensure all sponsored family members undergo the required medical examinations.
  • Keep all residency documents up to date.
  • Be aware of the conditions and validity of the sponsored visa.

Other Important Details:

The validity of a family-sponsored residence visa is usually one to three years and is linked to the sponsor's residence permit.
