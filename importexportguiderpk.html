Key Considerations:

  • Regulatory Framework: Familiarize yourself with the State Bank of Pakistan (SBP) and Federal Board of Revenue (FBR) guidelines.
  • Market Research: Identify viable products and target markets.
  • Logistics and Supply Chain: Plan your shipping, warehousing, and distribution.
  • Financial Planning: Secure adequate funding and understand payment mechanisms (e.g., Letters of Credit).

Import Business in Pakistan

Importing goods into Pakistan involves several steps and regulations. Key areas to focus on include:

  • Registration: Obtain a National Tax Number (NTN) and Sales Tax Registration Number (STRN). Register with the Web Based One Customs (WeBOC) system.
  • Import Policy Order: Understand the current Import Policy Order issued by the Ministry of Commerce, which lists permissible and restricted items.
  • Documentation:
    • Bill of Lading / Air Waybill
    • Commercial Invoice
    • Packing List
    • Certificate of Origin
    • Letter of Credit (L/C) or other payment documents
    • Import License (if applicable)
    • Insurance Certificate
  • Customs Clearance: File a Goods Declaration (GD) through WeBOC. Pay applicable duties and taxes (Customs Duty, Sales Tax, Income Tax, Additional Customs Duty, Regulatory Duty).
  • Major Imports: Petroleum products, machinery, raw cotton, plastic materials, iron & steel, electrical machinery, vehicles, and edible oils are among Pakistan's major imports.
  • Finding Suppliers: Utilize online B2B platforms, trade fairs, and industry associations to find reliable international suppliers.

Challenges can include fluctuating exchange rates, port congestion, and changes in import policies. Staying updated is crucial.

Export Business in Pakistan

Exporting from Pakistan offers significant growth opportunities. Key aspects include:

  • Registration: Similar to importing, NTN, STRN, and WeBOC registration are required. Exporters may also need to register with relevant trade bodies like the Trade Development Authority of Pakistan (TDAP).
  • Export Policy Order: Review the Export Policy Order for incentives, prohibited items, and procedural requirements.
  • Documentation:
    • Shipping Bill / Bill of Export
    • Commercial Invoice
    • Packing List
    • Certificate of Origin (sometimes GSP Certificate for preferential tariffs)
    • Bill of Lading / Air Waybill
    • Letter of Credit (L/C) or other payment documents
    • Quality Inspection Certificates (if required)
    • Export License (if applicable)
  • Key Export Sectors: Textiles and apparel, rice, leather goods, sports goods, surgical instruments, chemicals, fruits, and seafood are major exports.
  • Finding Buyers: Participate in international trade exhibitions, leverage online marketplaces, and connect with TDAP for support in finding overseas buyers.
  • Export Incentives: The government often provides various incentives like duty drawback schemes, export financing schemes, and subsidies to promote exports.

Exporters should focus on quality control, competitive pricing, and reliable logistics to succeed in international markets.
