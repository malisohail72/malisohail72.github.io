Frequently Asked Questions

How do I know if I need to file income tax in Pakistan?

In Pakistan, you need to file income tax if your annual income exceeds Rs. 600,000. However, different tax slabs apply based on your income level and profession. Salaried individuals, business owners, and freelancers have different filing requirements.

What are the best savings options for short-term goals in Pakistan?

For short-term goals (1-3 years), consider high-interest savings accounts, term deposit certificates from banks, short-term government securities, or National Savings schemes like Bahbood Savings Certificates or Defense Savings Certificates.

How can I start investing in Pakistan Stock Exchange with limited funds?

You can start investing in PSX with as little as Rs. 5,000-10,000. First, open an account with a broker, get a CDC sub-account, and complete KYC requirements. Consider starting with mutual funds which allow investment with smaller amounts before directly buying individual stocks.

What are the best online earning opportunities for Pakistanis?

Pakistanis can earn online through freelancing platforms like Fiverr, Upwork, and Freelancer. Popular skills include graphic design, content writing, web development, digital marketing, and virtual assistance. Additionally, content creation on YouTube, teaching English online, and e-commerce are viable options.

How does Islamic banking differ from conventional banking in Pakistan?

Islamic banking operates according to Shariah principles, which prohibit interest (riba), gambling (maysir), and excessive uncertainty (gharar). Instead of charging interest, Islamic banks earn through profit-sharing arrangements (Mudarabah), partnerships (Musharakah), cost-plus financing (Murabaha), and leasing (Ijara). All products are approved by Shariah advisors.
