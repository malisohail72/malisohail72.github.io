FAQ (Frequently Asked Questions)

Q: What is income tax?

A: Income tax is a tax levied by a government on the income of individuals or entities (taxpayers) with respect to their income or capital gains (realized or deemed realized) as mandated by the provisions of the relevant tax law.

Q: What are the different tax brackets for salaried individuals in Pakistan?

A: Tax brackets in Pakistan vary based on income levels and are subject to change by the Federal Board of Revenue (FBR). It's recommended to refer to the latest FBR guidelines for the most up-to-date information.

Q: How can I reduce my income tax liability?

A: You can potentially reduce your income tax liability through various deductions and tax credits available under the Pakistani tax laws. These might include investments, allowances, and other eligible expenses. Consult the FBR website or a tax professional for detailed guidance.

ℹ️ General Information

Tax Year: The tax year in Pakistan typically runs from July 1st to June 30th of the following year.

Filing Deadline: The deadline for filing income tax returns for salaried individuals is usually September 30th each year, but this can be extended by the FBR.

Tax Identification Number: Every taxpayer in Pakistan is required to have a National Tax Number (NTN).

Important Note: The calculators provided on this page are for estimation purposes only. For accurate tax calculations and filing, please consult the official guidelines of the Federal Board of Revenue (FBR) of Pakistan or a qualified tax professional.
